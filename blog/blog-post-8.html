7 Technologies that Companies should be paying attention to in 2022

The last few months have brought numerous changes to companies, from the way they work to the relationship with their suppliers and customers, with a view to adapting to the new realities imposed by the pandemic.

The use of digital tools has increased in all sectors, which have bet as never before on technological innovation, with the aim of responding to new challenges of everyday life, improving performance and ensuring the efficiency of services.

Given this scenario, there are several technological trends pointed out by different consultants as strategically critical to create or transform the business environment, some are gaining more maturity, others are beginning to emerge. They follow the trends that gather more consensus among the main consultants.


Top Emerging Technologies

  • Generative AI

    • The generative AI call refers to Artificial Intelligence algorithms that learn on their own to create new content similar to the original, from existing content such as text, audio, or image. Despite being still emerging, it is already being applied for different purposes, from identity protection, to the creation of high-resolution images from low-resolution images, through the transformation of computer-generated voice into "human" voice or, in the health area, by rendering prosthetic limbs, organic molecules and other items, created from the ground up through 3D printing, CRISPR and other technologies, or for the early identification of malignant potential, with a view to originating more effective treatment plans.

  • Hyperautomation

    • Hyperautomation is a targeted approach to quickly identify, examine, and automate as many business processes and Information Technology (IT" processes. With the help of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning and robotics, hyperautomation accelerates the ability to eliminate manual and bureaucratic tasks, minimize errors, and increase business productivity.

  • Homomorphic Encryption

    • Technologies such as Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) or differential privacy promise to simplify the mechanics of data sharing, while helping to keep individuals' information safe and anonymous. This enables sensitive data stored so far on servers around the world due to privacy or regulatory issues to begin generating value among companies in the form of new models and business opportunities. It is to be expected that more companies to explore new possibilities for secure and integrated data sharing, helping to monetize their own assets.

  • Cloud Native Platforms

    • The adoption of cloud native platforms will allow companies to make a leap in the availability of digital resources "anytime and anywhere", with faster return on investment and lower costs. By opting for a native development logic for the cloud, organizations benefit from the advantages of distributed computing, adopting more resilient, flexible and agile applications, which allow a rapid response of companies to business requirements, constantly changing. The strategy fits all areas where it is necessary to develop applications, from customer services to internal processes and business support tools.

  • Blockchain

    • The impact of blockchain seems to be only at the beginning. The technical advances and regulation have helped drive blockchain adoption beyond financial services. The maturing of this technology and related technologies has made progress in supporting interoperability, scalability and security and has contributed to changing the nature of the business, helping companies to rethink the creation and management of identity, data, brand, advertising, contracts, supply chains, professional certifications or copyright, among other tangible and digital assets. Blockchain is believed to usher in an era of true transparency in business transactions, an increasingly relevant element with the growth of digital payments.

  • Smart Spaces

    • Smart spaces are defined as physical or digital environments in which humans and technology systems interact in increasingly open, connected, coordinated, and intelligent ecosystems. The definition covers concepts such as smart cities, digital workspaces, or digital environments. As companies realize the ability of these spaces to incorporate legacy systems alongside new technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT), there will be more opportunities to integrate more connected and intelligent solutions into new environments.

  • Metaverse

    • Although the metaverse still has a long way to go to be established in the market, is a technology to keep in mind in the near future. This immersive digital environment will allow to provide persistent, decentralized, collaborative and interoperable digital content that intersects with physical content in real time and extends computing capacity to a much greater magnitude than that available today, changing the way individuals and businesses interact with each other and with the world.
