Mental Exhaustion - How to deal with work overload

With burnout cases growing day after day in during the pandemic, it is clear that we need to talk about work overload and emotional state that can lead to mental exhaustion.

While many of us take more work than we are able to endure for issues that overlap our desire, such as pressure from superiors, need to supplement wages with more than one job, or fear of termination, others just procrastinate until the last moment or take more work than they can deliberately endure.

Whatever the cause, several studies have shown that a considerable number of workers feel the increasing stress due to the demand for work and the more stressed we feel, the lower our productivity is.

Although the healthiest is obviously asking for reinforcement in activities or saying that you can't do with them, life isn't that easy.


How to deal with overload at work and avoid mental exhaustion?

Better Manage Your Time
You can write down all the tasks you need to do and evaluate them from 0 to 5, where 0 is the least important and 5 the top priority. After you do this, work with tasks on your list one by one, focusing first on the highest priority tasks.

Procrastination? Not here!
To solve the problem, identify all your time-saving habits by annotating everything you do during the workday when you're waiting what you need to do and try to change them. Remember that you can enjoy your days and weeks better if you're not doing everything as if it were a last-minute recipe seasoned with stress and with a few hints of tiredness.

Mental Exhaustion and Overload at Work
Talk to your superiors about your limits and be open about how far you can go without overloading yourself and harming yourself, your work, and your team. If they still insist on giving you high demand, do your best without crossing the line of your health and well-being. Remember that it is better to do one thing well done than two sloppy, since you will need to redo later.

Rest to Avoid Mental Exhaustion!
Even if it looks like your co-workers, bosses and clients can't handle everything without you, they can. In contrast, your family and friends cannot replace you in the joyful moments you lose by being overburdened at work, stressed and tired. If you have neglected your time of rest and leisure, it's time to stop. Take those vacations you've been putting off, take a break, enjoy the holidays and get off work. This will refresh you and make you more willing when you get back to work, which can improve your performance and productivity at work. Also, in the midst of the ethic work routine, take the time to treat your mental health as a priority: learn to meditate and slow down using breathing as a stress-fighting tool.
