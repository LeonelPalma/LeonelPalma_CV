Body Language: What does the body speak for you?

What is body language?

Body language is the set of all expressions and behaviors of the body, it groups the messages that you transmit beyond what is said in words.

Quinesica or Kinesics is the scientific field that deals with this form of interlocution, investigating and interpreting the meaning of body signals.

Diverse contexts and situations make our senses manifest themselves in peculiar ways. This means that while we think and make decisions our body responds to this also physically, either with a facial expression or even in the way we react to something.

It is interesting that, almost always, we do not realize this gestural behavior, which can reveal a lot about our feelings and personality.


Within body language, there are two types of posture:

  • Open — In the open posture there are no barriers between you and the person you are talking to, that is, they can understand what we are feeling and talking about, making the conversation smoother and more normal, which is best suited for most of our daily activities that require contact.
  • Closed — On the other hand, in the closed posture, there is the intention to protect and isolate the body, just as if it were an extinct "threat". Crossing the arms and legs demonstrates this intention and indicates resistance, fear or lack of comfort in relation to the theme treated. It is a common sign that the person is angry or rejects the situation he is going through.


    • How do I listen to my body?

  • Bite Lips

    Biting and chewing the lip usually indicates fear and anxiety. This may mean that the person is uncomfortable in the situation, worried or insecure about something, the lips can also be an indicator of stress during a lie. This is a very common action in children, especially if accompanied by raised eyebrows in the middle and lowered on the sides.

  • Crossing Arms or Legs

    Arms and legs crossed are physical barriers that suggest that the other person is not open to what he is saying. Psychologically, legs or crossed arms signal that a person is mentally, emotionally and physically blocked from what is in front of them. It's not intentional, and that's why it's so revealing.

  • Smile with your eyes closed

    A true smile, surrounds your whole face: mouth, ears, wrinkles around the eyes and among several other expressions. A fake smile, on the other hand, only includes lips, so to detect a true smile, all you need to do is look for wrinkles. People often smile to hide what they are really thinking and feeling. So the next time you want to know if someone's smile is genuine, look for wrinkles in the corners of your eyes. If they're not there, that smile is hiding something.


    • How does body language help me in my daily work?

    Body language takes the lead when it comes to human communication. That is, if you want to have a successful career, it is to be aware of some attitudes that can open the door to a successful trajectory. This applies both to the pursuit of a promotion and to a job interview.

  • Make synchronized moves with your team

    A team that makes the same movements, giving the same signs with the feet, hands and head tends to find better solutions to the problems. By maintaining the uniformity of body language, the team can connect and think about innovative proposals.

  • Relax the muscles of the face

    When checking your emails, regardless of content try to preserve relaxed facial muscles. Our expressions, including the simple act of frowning, often reveal the information we receive, and can create embarrassing situations.

  • Sit upright

    Maintaining a good posture facilitates the process of professional ascent. Your way of sitting reflects the care you put into what you are doing and can earn you more praise on the tasks performed.

  • Keep your voice tone down

    Speaking in low tone causes a more pleasant feeling in the corporate environment, in addition to passing more authority. When leadership is natural, you don't need screams to reinforce it.

  • Always greet people

    Handshakes and pats on the back give the feeling of complicity, cooperation and animation, as well as increases trust between people. Cherish a more harmonious environment and never forget to greet the people who will make a deal with you.
